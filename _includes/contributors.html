Contributors

Thanks to the following for contributing to the development of these sequences:

  • Dr Olivia has a PhD in social anthropology which took her to work among the BaAka hunter-gatherers of the Congo basin rainforest. She now lives in north-east Victoria with her family, and hopes to begin her teaching career in 2018.

  • Dr Nicola Munro spent four years at Roxby Downs, outback South Australia, helping to establish the Arid Recovery reserve. She built feral-animal-proof fences and got rid of rabbits, cats and foxes from inside the reserve and was involved with reintroduction programs for locally extinct mammals such as bilbies and bettongs. She also monitored the changes in vegetation when you return the original suit of animals. Nicola then completed a PhD in restoration ecology, looking at the capacity of revegetation in agricultural landscapes to provide habitat. She recently researched the digging behaviour of bettongs in grassy woodlands near Canberra, to see if they have the potential to change entire vegetation communities.
